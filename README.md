# Guide for Writing Data Management Plans

> Please check out the [relevant issue](https://github.com/Comunita-Italiana-Data-Steward/DMP-guide/issues/5).

The aim of this project is to create an accessible, practical but complete guide on writing a [Data Management Plan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_management_plan) (DMP).

This guide is useful to:
- Researchers wishing or needing to create a DMP for their projects;
- Research Data Managers and Data Stewards, needing material to support their researchers;
- Data Stewards-in-training wishing to learn how to write a DMP.

## About

There are several tools available online to support those wishing to create a DMP.
This one is in the form of a manual, providing context, practical excercises and examples.
By reading the guide from top to bottom, the reader should learn all there is to know about DMPs and how to manage their research data.

The guide assumes that the reader can access the help of a Data Management expert, like a Data Steward, to customize and fine-tune their Research Data Management (RDM) strategy.

Furthermore, the guide---for obvious reasons---cannot give legal advice on the creation, handling and dissemination of personal, particular on sensitive data.
Therefore, the guide assumes that the reader has access to and can be supported by legal and privacy experts.

The guide caters to the following groups of stakeholders:
- Researchers, doctoral students and professors wishing to create a DMP for their projects;
- Team leads and Lab heads wishing to create a DMP for their laboratories/research groups;
- Winners of large funding grants mandating the creation of Data Management Plans;
- Data Stewards or Research Managers needing material to distribute to the people they are supporting;
- Data Stewards in training needing to learn how to create a Data Management Plan;

This guide was developed together with the [DMP Evaluation Framework](https://github.com/Comunita-Italiana-Data-Steward/DMP-evaluation-framework), present in another repository.
The general idea was to co-create a *DMP desiderata* in the evaluation framework, and a guide to help researchers fulfill these *desiderata*.

### Authors
The guide was initially written by [Luca Visentin](https://orcid.org/0000-0003-2568-5694), with the support of some staff members of the Politecnico di Milano.
Then, the guide was adopted by the CIDS for further development and refinement.

Everyone who made a significant contribution to the guide will be included in the edition notice page in the guide itself.
In any case, all contributions, no matter how small, are included at [the bottom of this README](#all-contributors).

## Contributing to the guide
The guide is open for contributions from everyone!
Please refer to the [Contributing Guide](meta/CONTRIBUTING.md) for more information.

Contributors are expected to follow the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) of this repository.

> [!TIP]
> You don't need any particular experience to contribute!
> We accept and treasure contributions also from non-experts in the field of DMP writing, such as fixing typos, providing feedback, and more.
> Refer to the contributing guide to learn more.
> Thank you!
